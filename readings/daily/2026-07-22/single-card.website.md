*attachment, bondage, materialism, shadow*

you're looking at a card that doesn't bluff. the devil shows up when you've got your hands wrapped around something that's been squeezing back — a pattern, a person, a promise you made to yourself that turned into a leash. it's not evil, not in the fire-and-brimstone sense. it's the part of you that knows the gold chain is still a chain, even when it's pretty. you've been telling yourself you *need* this. the devil wants you to look at that sentence again. need, or habit? need, or fear of what happens if you let go? this card doesn't ask you to burn anything down. it asks you to notice your hands are full.

## the sky

**Sun in Leo** — the ego wants what it wants, and the devil knows how to dress up desire as destiny.

**Moon in Taurus** — comfort becomes a cage when you'd rather feel safe than feel free.

## what it means together

the sun in leo is all about performance — showing up, being seen, claiming your power. the devil in the same room means you're performing for an audience of one, and that one is the voice in your head that says *you can't afford to lose this*. the moon in taurus wants security, wants the familiar, wants to keep the fridge full and the routine intact. the devil nods along. it says *stay. this is fine. you've worked for this.* and it's not lying — you have worked for this. but the work might have been building a cell you can live in, not a door you can walk through.

what you're attached to right now isn't the thing itself. it's the story you've attached to the thing. the devil is the card of the golden handcuffs — and the moment you see them as handcuffs, you're already halfway out. you don't have to wrench yourself free today. just look down at your wrists. ask: *is this holding me, or am I holding it?*
