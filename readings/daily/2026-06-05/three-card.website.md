*acceptance, healing, forgiveness, moving on · emotional overwhelm, codependency, martyrdom, insecurity · overwhelm, confusion, exposed feelings, no peace*

Let's start with where you've been, because that's what's still humming under everything. The Five of Cups reversed means you've already done the hardest part — you turned around from the spilled cups and saw the two still standing. That's not nothing. That's the difference between staying in the wreckage and understanding the wreckage happened. You've been working on letting go of something that asked for more grief than it deserved, and you've gotten good enough at it that the pain doesn't run the show anymore. But here's the thing about healing: it doesn't make you waterproof.

The Queen of Cups reversed is sitting in your present, and she's not here to be kind to you. She's here to show you where your compassion has turned into a liability — where you're giving so much emotional bandwidth to someone else that your own cup is running on fumes. You know that feeling when you're trying to be the soft landing for everyone else, but nobody's holding the blanket for you? That's this card. The intuition is still there, the empathy is still real, but the boundaries have gone soft. You're taking on water because you're not sure where you end and someone else's feelings begin.

And then there's the Two of Swords reversed in the future position, which is the part that might actually sting: the stalemate you've been maintaining — the careful not-looking, the deliberate distance from a hard truth — it's about to crack. Not because you want it to. Because the pressure has been building while you were busy being everyone's emotional sponge, and the blindfold is slipping. You're going to see something you've been avoiding, and it's going to arrive as a kind of overwhelm that doesn't ask permission.

This morning's Eight of Wands set the speed for all of this — fast. Whatever's coming is already in motion.

## the sky

**Mercury in Gemini (10th house)** — Mercury is at home in Gemini, sharp and communicative, and in the 10th house it wants career, reputation, public-facing clarity — so the universe is asking you to say the thing out loud, professionally or otherwise, before the pressure builds any further.

**Venus in Taurus (5th house)** — Venus is also at home in Taurus, grounded and sensual, and in the 5th house it's asking you to remember what pleasure feels like when you're not performing it for someone else's comfort — your joy is not a resource to be extracted.

**Mars in Leo (7th house)** — Mars in Leo wants to fight for what it loves, and in the 7th house that means relationships are about to become arenas — not hostile, but honest, and you will need to show up as your full self rather than the version that's easier to digest.

## the thread

Here's what the cards and the sky agree on: you've been holding too much, and the container is cracking. The Five of Cups reversed says you've already grieved the big thing. The Queen of Cups reversed says you're still bleeding from the small cuts — the daily boundary violations, the emotional labor you're not clocking, the way you've made yourself indispensable to people who wouldn't notice if you stopped showing up. And the Two of Swords reversed says the denial about all of this is about to become unsustainable. Mercury in Gemini in the 10th house is practically shouting: say it. Say what you need. Say what you can't carry anymore. Say it in a meeting, in a text, in a conversation you've been postponing for weeks.

Venus in Taurus in the 5th is the counterweight — it's reminding you that your capacity for pleasure, for rest, for doing something just because it feels good, is not a luxury you earn after everyone else is taken care of. It's a requirement. And Mars in Leo in the 7th is the ignition: if you don't speak your truth soon, the relationship will speak it for you, and it won't be gentle.

The Eight of Wands this morning was a warning dressed as momentum. Things are moving. The question isn't whether you'll be ready — it's whether you'll let the movement carry you or brace against it until something breaks.

You already turned around from the grief. Now you have to turn toward the truth.
