*manipulation, illusion, scattered energy, untapped talent, smugness, excess, shallow pleasure, dissatisfaction, ruin, endings, betrayal, collapse*

you're coming from a place where your talents may have been unfocused, or worse, used to manipulate others — The Magician in reverse can be a warning that you've been all talk, and it's time to actually embody your potential. this can be a tough pill to swallow, especially when it seems like you've been doing just fine. but the truth is, you may have been using your gifts to create illusions, rather than actual progress. and now, in the present, you're dealing with the aftermath of those choices — the Nine of Cups in reverse suggests that your pleasures may be excessive, or performative, and not actually fulfilling. it's like you're trying to fill a void, but using all the wrong things to do it. and then, of course, there's the future — the Ten of Swords is a stark, painful ending. it's collapse, betrayal, and total mental exhaustion. but it's also a new beginning, in a way. the worst has already happened, and now you get to start over.

it's a lot to take in, especially when you consider the undertone set by this morning's single card — the Knight of Cups. romance, charm, all that jazz. it's like you were feeling all the feels, and then reality set in. and now you're left to deal with the consequences of your actions. 
you're exhausted. 
you're done. 

## the sky
**the moon in virgo** wants things accounted for — it's time to get real about your emotions, and your actions. **venus in leo** is all about drama, and self-expression — but it can also be a bit of a show-off. and **mars in taurus** is just trying to get things done, in a practical, no-nonsense kind of way. 

## the thread
so, how do these three cards relate to each other? well, for starters, it's clear that you've been using your gifts in ways that aren't entirely authentic. The Magician in reverse is a big red flag, waving in the air, saying "hey, get it together!" and then the Nine of Cups in reverse comes along, and it's like, "yeah, you're not even enjoying the things you think you are." it's a big wake-up call, in other words. and then, of course, there's the Ten of Swords — which is just, well, a mess. but it's also a chance to start over, to rebuild from the ground up. 
the thing is, you can't do that without being honest with yourself. you can't just keep using your gifts to manipulate others, or to create illusions. you have to get real, and you have to get practical. that's where the moon in virgo comes in — it's time to get things accounted for, to make a plan, and to stick to it. and yeah, it might not be the most glamorous thing in the world, but it's necessary. 
you're at a crossroads, in other words. you can keep on keeping on, using your gifts in ways that aren't entirely authentic — or you can take a deep breath, and start over. it's not going to be easy, but it's going to be worth it. 
you're ready. 
you're done waiting.
