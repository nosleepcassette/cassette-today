*stagnation, boredom, stubbornness, laziness, perfectionism*

There’s a difference between steady and stuck, and you’ve been feeling the difference lately. The Knight of Pentacles reversed isn’t about quitting — it’s about grinding in place. You’re still showing up, still doing the thing, but the thing has lost its texture. Every step feels like the last step, and the horizon hasn’t moved in weeks. The work gets done, but not the way it used to. Not with any kind of curiosity or care. Just motion, because stopping would mean admitting something’s off.

The reversed Knight hoards his energy like it’s the last ration in a siege. But the siege is imaginary. You’re not conserving strength — you’re refusing to redistribute it. Perfectionism here is a cage dressed as discipline. You’re triple-checking tasks that don’t need checking, avoiding the new thing because the old thing still has loose ends. Boredom isn’t a signal to work harder. It’s a signal that the route needs changing.

## the sky

No major transits today — the sky offers no distractions, just the weight of your own choices. The planets aren’t pushing you forward or holding you back. That’s yours.

## what it means together

When the sky goes quiet and the Knight of Pentacles shows up reversed, the stagnation becomes yours to own. There’s no cosmic excuse, no retrograde to blame. The resistance you feel isn’t coming from outside — it’s the friction between what you’re doing and what you actually want to be doing. You’ve been treating a rut as loyalty. But steady doesn’t mean immobile. The Knight reversed asks you to stop polishing the same spot and look at the rest of the field.

The boredom is information. The stubbornness is armor you don’t need anymore. Ask yourself: what would happen if you put the half-finished project down and started something you actually wanted to do? Not abandoned — just set aside. The Knight of Pentacles upright knows when to hold. Reversed, he forgets when to release. You don’t have to burn it all down. But you do have to move your feet.
