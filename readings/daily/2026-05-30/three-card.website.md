*acceptance, healing, forgiveness · tension at home, instability, incomplete milestone · withdrawal, quest, disappointment*

The past is turning its pockets out. Five of Cups reversed doesn't mean the grief is gone — it means the grief has stopped being the only thing you can see. You're starting to notice what didn't spill. What stayed. And that shift, small as it feels, is the actual hinge this whole spread turns on.

But the present isn't letting you rest there. Four of Wands reversed is the party that didn't happen. The home that feels less like shelter and more like negotiation. Whatever milestone you thought you'd reached — the anniversary, the truce, the moment you could finally exhale — it's wobbling. Not collapsed, just not solid enough to lean on. You're still building the table while people are already sitting down to eat.

And then the future: Eight of Cups walking away. Not slamming doors. Just walking, quietly, toward the door that's been open the whole time. This card doesn't storm off — it leaves because staying would cost more than leaving. The disappointment here isn't fresh. It's old, worn smooth, finally acknowledged.

The Nine of Pentacles from this morning's single card is the undertone you can't shake: you've built something real. Something you can touch. That independence isn't theoretical — it's in how you've learned to hold your own weight. But the Eight of Cups is asking if the thing you built is still the thing you want to live inside.

## the sky

**Saturn Retrograde in Pisces (2nd house)** — the limits you thought were permanent are softening, but only so you can rebuild them from scratch, with your own hands.

**Mars in Leo (9th house)** — you're fighting for the right to believe something different, even if it means burning down the old maps.

**Venus in Taurus (6th house)** — small pleasures are the anchor right now: a good meal, a finished task, the body remembering it deserves care.

## the thread

Here's the shape of it: you've done the hard work of letting grief open into something livable. That's real. That's the Five of Cups reversed — not forgetting, but no longer drowning. But the Four of Wands reversed says the structures you were counting on to hold that new peace aren't ready yet. The home you thought you'd come back to is still under construction. And the Eight of Cups says you already know, somewhere quiet, that you can't wait for it to be finished.

You're not leaving because you're broken. You're leaving because you've healed enough to feel the gap between what you have and what you need.

The Saturn retrograde in Pisces in your 2nd house is the slow dissolve of old limits around worth and value — not just money, but what you believe you deserve. The old rules about what's possible are losing their grip. Mars in Leo in the 9th is the fire that says: I get to want a different story. And Venus in Taurus in the 6th is the counterweight — not a grand vision, just the daily practice of treating yourself like someone worth tending.

The Nine of Pentacles undertone is the quiet truth: you've already built enough to leave from. You're not walking into nothing. You're walking out of something that stopped fitting.

The thread is this: the grief that opened you, the home that wobbled, and the door you're walking through are all the same journey. You don't leave because you failed. You leave because you finally know what you're looking for.
