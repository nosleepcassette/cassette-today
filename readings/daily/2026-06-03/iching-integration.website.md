**Multitude (#7)**

earth over water. the receptive holding the abysmal. the image: in the middle of the earth is water — the army. a reservoir beneath the soil, unseen but felt when you dig. the superior man increases his masses by generosity toward the people. this is not a card about war. this is a card about what happens when enough people move in the same direction.

*The army needs perseverance and a strong man. Good fortune without blame.*

the judgment is precise: the army needs organization, not aggression. a multitude without structure is a mob. a multitude with structure is a force. the strong man here isn't a dictator — it's the person who can hold the center while everyone else moves around them. the one who doesn't flinch when the noise rises. the one who keeps the water in the earth instead of letting it flood.

hexagram 7 names a time of collective action. you are not alone in this moment, and that's the point. whatever you're facing, it requires coordination. delegation. trust that other people will do their part so you can do yours. the posture demanded is not heroic — it's managerial. not in the corporate sense, but in the ancient sense: you are the one who sees the whole field and knows where each unit needs to stand.

## the changing lines

**Line 3** — *"A corpse in the army. Misfortune."*

this is the warning you didn't ask for but absolutely need. a corpse in the army means someone who occupies a position without contributing. dead weight. the person who holds rank but has checked out. the line doesn't say whose corpse it is — could be someone else, could be you. look at where you're going through the motions. look at where you're present in title only. the army cannot afford passengers right now. if you're not bringing something, step aside so someone who will can take the position.

**Line 6** — *"The great prince issues commands, founds states, vests families with fiefs. Inferior men should not be employed."*

the closing line. the one that lands after the work is done. the great prince — that's you, after you've held the center — issues commands that outlast the immediate crisis. founds states. vests families with fiefs. this is about building structures that endure. but the warning is sharp: inferior men should not be employed. don't reward the wrong people just because the crisis is over. don't let the ones who carried nothing collect the spoils. the post-battle settlement determines whether peace holds or the next war starts.

these two lines together: line 3 asks who's dead weight, and line 6 asks who you're trusting with the future. they're the same question at different stages. clear the corpse before you hand out the fiefs.

## the relating hexagram

**Return (#24)** — 復

the army, after the campaign, comes home. hexagram 7 moves toward hexagram 24 — return, the turning point, the solstice moment when light begins its slow crawl back. the multitude disperses back into individuals. the collective action resolves into personal restoration.

this is where the day is going: not into another battle, but into the quiet aftermath. the return. the moment you put down the armor and remember who you are without the uniform. hexagram 7 asks you to organize and lead. hexagram 24 asks you to come back to yourself when it's done. both are required. neither works without the other.

## synthesis

the morning's card was the queen of pentacles. grounded. sensible. the one who keeps the household running while the world burns. she's the practical counterpart to hexagram 7's general — she's not on the battlefield, she's making sure there's food when the soldiers come home. the queen of pentacles says: don't lose the material reality while you're organizing the multitude. someone has to tend the fire. someone has to count the stores. that's you, today.

the hexagram sees the structural layer underneath all of this. the chariot was individual will. the queen of pentacles is individual stewardship. but hexagram 7 says: you are not doing this alone. the three of wands shows you scanning the horizon alone, but the army is behind you. the wheel of fortune turns for everyone, not just you.

where the tarot and the i ching confirm each other: the queen of pentacles and line 3's warning about the corpse in the army. both say: check the resources. check who's actually contributing. the queen won't tolerate dead weight in her household, and the army won't carry it either. where they complicate each other: the wheel of fortune suggests fate, randomness, things outside your control. hexagram 7 says: organize anyway. fate doesn't excuse you from doing the work. the wheel turns whether you're ready or not — be ready.

what this means practically: today, you're the one who holds the center. not because you're the loudest or the most powerful, but because you see the whole field. your job is to coordinate, not to do everything yourself. your job is to clear the dead weight — gently, but clearly — and then build structures that outlast the current crisis. the army needs perseverance, and a strong man. the strong man is you. but the army is everyone else. let them carry their share.

and when the campaign is over — and it will be, because hexagram 24 is waiting — come home. the return is as important as the march.
