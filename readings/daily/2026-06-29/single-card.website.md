*bondage, attachment, materialism, temptation, shadow*

The Devil doesn't arrive with horns and a pitchfork — he arrives with a familiar ache. That thing you know isn't good for you, the habit you've rationalized into a need, the person whose chaos you've mistaken for passion. The card shows chains that could slip off any time, but nobody's looking at the chains. You're looking at what you think you can't live without.

This is not a card about evil. It's a card about the particular misery of wanting something that costs more than it gives. The Devil's domain is the stuff you'd be embarrassed to admit you still cling to — the phone you pick up when you're lonely, the argument you keep having, the job that pays for your life but drains it. The chains are real. They're also optional. That's the part that stings.

## the sky

No major transits today. The sky is quiet, which means the Devil's spotlight is on you. No cosmic distractions, no planetary excuses. Just the raw architecture of your own attachments, waiting to be named.

## what it means together

A quiet sky and the Devil is a dangerous combination — not because something bad will happen, but because you'll have to sit with what you already know. The transit silence removes the option of blaming the moon or mercury retrograde for your patterns. The Devil doesn't need a cosmic stage. He works best in the small, repetitive choices you make when nobody's watching.

Here's the thing about this card in a clear sky: it's actually more hopeful than it looks. The Devil's chains have slack. They always do. The bondage is maintained by agreement, not force. A quiet day like today is good for inventory — what are you holding that's holding you back? What would you lose if you set it down? Not the thing itself, but the story you've built around needing it.

The Devil wants you to think the deal is permanent. It's not. The sky isn't conspiring against you. It's just waiting to see what you'll do with the space.
