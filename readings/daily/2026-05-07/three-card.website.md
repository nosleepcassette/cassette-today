## the spread

**Past: Wheel of Fortune · Present: Six of Swords · Future: The Moon**

*the morning's single card: three of wands reversed — delays, obstacles, frustration, stalled growth, lack of foresight*

the wheel in your past position says you already went through something you couldn't control. a turn, a shift, something that reorganized the board while you were still learning the rules of the old game. whatever happened — and you know what it was — it wasn't personal, but it was thorough. the wheel doesn't spin for your benefit or your ruin; it spins because that's what it does. the important part is that it already happened. you're past it in the timeline even if your nervous system hasn't caught up yet.

the six of swords in the present is the card of getting out. moving away from choppy water toward calmer water. being ferried. but — and this is the part people skip — you're still on the boat. you haven't arrived. the present is transitional. you're mid-crossing, and mid-crossing is one of the hardest places to be because you can't go back and you can't quite see the shore. the six of swords says you made the right call to leave. it does not say the crossing is over.

and then the moon. the moon in the future position is not the card you want to see if you were hoping for arrival, clarity, or resolution. the moon is the card of what you can't see yet — not because it's hidden from you, but because it's still forming. the moon is the realm of the uncanny, the intuitive, the thing you sense before you can name it. after a crossing, you don't land on solid ground — you land in a place where you'll have to feel your way forward. that's not failure. that's just what comes after leaving.

the morning's three of wands reversed hangs over all three of these cards like a weather system. the delay you're feeling right now — the stalled ship, the plan that won't launch — it's not separate from this spread. it IS this spread. you're in the delay. the wheel turned, you got on the boat, and now you're sitting in the middle of the water watching the fog roll in. of course the wands reversed showed up this morning. of course it feels like nothing's moving.

## the sky

**Sun in Taurus** (10th house) — the public layer, your reputation, your standing-in-the-world zone is held by the most deliberate sign in the zodiac. whatever you're building visibly, it wants to be slow and real. anything fast in the 10th right now is probably unstable.

**Moon in Capricorn** (6th house) — your emotional attention is on the daily grind, the routines, the maintenance tasks that keep the lights on. capricorn moon in the 6th means you're probably working harder than you feel like admitting, and the work is the kind nobody gives you credit for.

**Mercury in Taurus** (10th house) — your thinking is fixed on career and public matters, and it's not moving fast. communication in professional contexts is slow, thorough, possibly stuck. nobody is being flexible at the office (literal or metaphorical).

## synthesis

here's the shape of today: the wheel already turned (past), you're mid-transit (present), and where you're heading isn't going to be clear anytime soon (future). meanwhile the sky has sun and mercury in taurus in your 10th house — the public, visible, accountable layer — which means everyone can see you not having arrived yet, and you're very aware they can see it.

the six of swords is doing the heaviest lifting in this spread. it's the hinge. the wheel happened, the moon is coming, but the six of swords is where you actually are: between, being ferried, not in charge of the boat. and with moon in capricorn in the 6th, the daily effort of just keeping yourself going while you're mid-crossing is real work. the kind you do without applause.

the three of wands reversed from this morning is the frustrated version of this — the "why aren't we there yet" energy. the spread says: because you're on a boat, not a teleporter. the moon says: and when you do arrive, it won't look like what you expected. that's not a threat. that's just how new territory works. you feel your way. you let your eyes adjust.
