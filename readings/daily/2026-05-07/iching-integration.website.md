## hexagram 42: Increasing (益)

**益 · Increasing**

*superior power from below, sacrifice of the upper to nourish the lower, gain that comes through giving away*

the judgment: "increasing. it furthers one to undertake something. it furthers one to cross the great water."

that second line is doing the work. "it furthers one to cross the great water" — this is not a hexagram about accumulating. this is a hexagram about the kind of gain that only happens when you commit to a crossing. the image is wind over thunder: movement below, gentleness above. something is stirring at the root level while the upper layers stay soft, receptive, willing to be shaped.

the structure of 益 tells you everything: the upper trigram (xun/wind) has a solid line at the bottom that doesn't belong to it — it's been borrowed from the lower trigram, which gave up a strong line and received a yielding one in return. the lower trigram (zhen/thunder) got lighter, more receptive, more open. the upper got heavier, more capable of action. increase isn't about getting more — it's about the right energy moving to where it's needed. the upper sacrifices to nourish the lower. the strong descends. the weak ascends. the system gains.

this hexagram is not about your profit. it's about the profit of the situation — which may or may not look like profit to you in the short term.

**Line 1 changing:**

"it furthers one to undertake great things. supreme good fortune. no blame."

the first line is at the very bottom of the hexagram — the root, the seed, the place where nothing has emerged yet. and it says: do the big thing. not later, not when you're more prepared. now. this is the line of someone who has barely started and is already being told to go bigger than feels reasonable. the "no blame" is important — it means even if it doesn't work out the way you picture, you're not wrong for trying. the energy at the bottom of increasing is pure potential. if you're going to use it, you use it all.

when this line changes, the hexagram becomes watching (观) — hexagram 20. the destination. watching is the tower, the detached observation, the ritual space where you stand still and see clearly. the arc from line 1 of increasing to watching is: throw yourself at the big thing, and what you gain is the capacity to see. action first, clarity after. the watching doesn't come before the attempt. it comes because of it.

## the tarot in context

the morning's three of wands reversed — the stalled ship, the delay, the frustration — is exactly what increasing feels like from the outside when you don't know the energy is moving below the surface. the three of wands reversed says nothing's happening. increasing says everything's happening, just underground. these aren't contradictory. they're the same event seen from different altitudes.

the three-card spread fills in the timeline: the wheel already turned (something broke or shifted out of your control), you're mid-crossing in the six of swords (being ferried, not driving), and the moon is waiting at the destination (you'll have to feel your way, not think your way). increasing is the hexagram that makes sense of all three positions — the wheel was the upper trigram giving up its strong line. the six of swords is the crossing that the judgment told you to make. the moon is the arrival into territory that requires exactly the kind of intuitive, low-visibility navigation that watching teaches.

## synthesis

sun and mercury in taurus in the 9th house under increasing — this is a day where your convictions get heavier, not lighter. the 9th house is where you look for meaning, and taurus is the sign that says "show me something I can stand on." increasing says: what you stand on is about to get stronger, but not because you acquired something — because something was given up. somewhere in your system, the upper layer is sacrificing for the lower. somewhere you're letting go of control, intensity, solidity at the top so that the root can take.

the moon in capricorn in the 5th is the emotional complication: whatever you're creating or risking today, you're approaching it with the gravity of someone who knows the stakes. this isn't light play. but increasing's line 1 says do the big thing anyway. the "no blame" is there specifically for the version of you who's about to talk yourself out of it because it doesn't feel safe enough yet.

the three of wands reversed from this morning was the surface reading — delay, frustration. increasing is the depth reading — the delay is the cure. the stall is the seed taking root. the relating hexagram, watching, is where this goes: after you commit to the big thing, after you cross the water, you arrive at a place where you can finally see. not because the fog lifts, but because you learned to see in fog. the moon in the three-card future position is the same terrain as watching — low visibility, high intuition, the kind of knowing that doesn't come from data.
