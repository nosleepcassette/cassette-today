**Multitude (#7)** 
_trigram pairing: earth over water, keywords: multitude, earth, water, the receptive_
the image of multitude describes a situation where water is in the middle of the earth — this isn't just a geographical or literal description, but an instruction on how to approach the current situation: by being grounded and nourishing, like the earth, and flowing, like the water, we can find a sense of community and shared purpose. 
_the judgment_ says: "_the army. the army needs perseverance and a strong man. good fortune without blame_" — on the surface, this is about the need for leadership and persistence in the face of challenges, but it's also about recognizing that a collective effort, a multitude, is necessary for success, and that this success is not just about achieving a goal, but about doing so in a way that is honorable and blameless. 
multitude names a time when we are part of something larger than ourselves, a collective effort that requires organization and leadership to become effective — it's a time for perseverance, for working together towards a common goal, and for recognizing the importance of strong, wise leadership. the posture it demands is one of cooperation, mutual support, and a willingness to work together, even when things get tough. 
what multitude sees about the situation is that it requires a balance between the individual and the collective — it's not just about personal achievement, but about how that achievement contributes to the greater good, and how the collective effort can support and uplift the individual.

## the sky
venus in taurus — the earthy, practical side of love and relationships is highlighted, and we're reminded that our connections with others are rooted in the physical world, and that beauty and joy can be found in the simple, everyday things. 
mars in cancer — our energies and motivations are closely tied to our emotions and our sense of security, and we may find ourselves feeling more protective and defensive than usual, but also more nurturing and caring. 
jupiter in aries — our desire for expansion and growth is ignited, and we're feeling more impulsive and adventurous than usual, but we need to be careful not to let our enthusiasm get ahead of our better judgment.

the hexagram, multitude, shows the structural layer underneath both — the need for collective effort, for perseverance, and for strong leadership. 
what's interesting is how the tarot and the i ching see the same thing from different angles: the fool's leap of faith is echoed in the multitude's need for perseverance and strong leadership; the devil's release from bondage is reflected in the multitude's emphasis on collective effort and mutual support; and the star's hope and inspiration are grounded in the multitude's recognition of the importance of nourishing and flowing like the earth and water. 
where they complicate or add nuance to each other is in the details: the tarot suggests a more individual, psychological focus, while the i ching emphasizes the collective, structural level; the tarot shows a more dramatic, symbolic landscape, while the i ching presents a more practical, down-to-earth perspective. 
practically, this means that we need to find a balance between our individual aspirations and the collective effort — we need to be willing to work together, to support and uplift each other, while also staying true to our own unique paths and purposes. 
it means that we need to be persevering, persistent, and strong, like the earth and water, and that we need to be open to the possibilities and uncertainties of the future, like the fool. 
it means that we need to let go of our fears and illusions, and find clarity and illumination in the darkness, like the moon reversed. 
and it means that we need to stay grounded, nourished, and flowing, like the earth and water, and that we need to recognize the importance of beauty, joy, and love in our lives, like venus in taurus.
