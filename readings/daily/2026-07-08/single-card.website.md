*isolation, withdrawal, loneliness, avoidance, misguidance*

You have been up on that mountain for a while now. The Hermit reversed is the lantern starting to flicker — not because you’ve run out of fuel, but because you’ve been holding it so long your hand has gone numb. The solitude that once felt like sanctuary has started to feel like a sentence. You went inward to find something, and you did find it — but now you’re sitting on it instead of carrying it back down. The reversed Hermit is wisdom that’s gone feral from disuse. It’s the knowing you refuse to share because sharing would mean re-entering the mess of other people. And maybe you’re afraid the wisdom isn’t actually finished yet — that if you come down, you’ll realize you still have more to learn, and that the mountain was just a very quiet way to avoid being wrong in front of anyone.

## the sky

**No major transits today** — the sky is quiet, offering no external push or pull.

Sometimes the absence of planetary pressure is itself a transit. Nothing is forcing you out of the cave. Nothing is calling you back. The only motion is the one you decide to make.

## what it means together

The Hermit reversed against a still sky is a stark invitation. There is no cosmic nudge here — no Mercury stationing direct to give you an excuse, no Mars aspect to light a fire under you. The quiet you’ve been sitting in is now just quiet. It no longer hums with the promise of revelation. It’s empty. And that emptiness is the only message: the mountain has given you everything it’s going to give you. What remains is the descent.

You know what you know. The question is whether you’ll bring it back to the village or let it fossilize in your hands. The reversed Hermit doesn’t mean the wisdom was wrong — it means the wisdom is waiting for a witness. Someone to test it against. Someone to argue with it. Someone to prove you wrong, maybe, so you can find out what else is true.

The sky offers no weather. That’s the point. You can’t blame the planets for your hesitation today. You can’t wait for a better time to come down. The path is clear, the air is still, and the only thing keeping you on that mountain is the belief that you’re not ready to be seen yet. You are. You’ve been ready. The lantern is for the road, not for the cave.
