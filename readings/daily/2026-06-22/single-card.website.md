*excess, imbalance, impatience, discord, overindulgence*

You've been trying to force a mixture that doesn't want to blend — and the more you push, the more it separates. Temperance reversed isn't about a catastrophic failure. It's the moment you realize you've added too much of one thing and not enough of another, and now the whole batch tastes wrong.

This is a card of proportion, and when it's upside down, proportion has left the building. Maybe you've been working too hard without recovery. Maybe you've been giving too much to a relationship that asks for less. Maybe you've been drinking from a well that's already dry, hoping the next sip will finally satisfy. The angel on the card is usually pouring water between cups — a steady, patient exchange. Reversed, that stream is splashing everywhere, missing both vessels entirely.

The fix isn't more effort. It's not a harder pour or a better angle. The fix is to stop pouring and look at what's already in the cups. What's actually in your hands right now, not what you wish was there. Temperance reversed asks you to take inventory before you add anything else.

## the sky

No major transits shift the sky today — the planets move through their quiet hum, and the moment asks you to sit with what is rather than what's coming next.

## what it means together

No transit to lean on means no external momentum to blame or thank. The imbalance is yours. That's not a judgment — it's a location. You're standing at the center of a mixture that needs recalibrating, not rescuing.

Here's the wry truth: you probably already know where the excess is. The thing you do a little too much of, the person you give a little too much to, the story you tell yourself a little too often. Temperance reversed doesn't reveal anything new. It just holds up a mirror and says *look at your hands.* They're full. Put something down before you pick anything else up.

The angel will wait. The cups aren't going anywhere. You get to choose what stays and what drains.
