**Retreat (#33)**

Heaven above, mountain below. The creative pushing upward, the fixed rising to meet it. The image is straightforward: mountain under heaven. You cannot hide a mountain from the sky, but you can let the sky see what it already knows.

*RETREAT. Success. In what is small, perseverance furthers.*

The judgment cuts clean — this is not failure. Retreat is a named strategy, not a concession. The I Ching does not say "hold the line." It says retreat succeeds. The small thing, the careful thing, the thing you do with your hands instead of your voice — that's where perseverance goes. Not in the grand stand. In the small.

The superior man keeps the inferior man at a distance, not angrily but with reserve. That's the posture. No heat. No performance of distance. Just space, cleanly held.

---

**The changing lines**

**Line 3** — *A halted retreat is nerve-wracking and dangerous.*

This is the line that wants to stay. The retreat that stops halfway, looks back, second-guesses. The danger isn't in the retreat itself — it's in the halt. You feel the nerve-wracking because you know you should keep moving but something in you wants to pretend the retreat isn't necessary. The danger is real. Not the retreat. The hesitation.

**Line 4** — *Voluntary retreat brings good fortune to the superior man and downfall to the inferior man.*

The difference is whether you choose it or get dragged into it. Voluntary retreat — the kind where you see the shape of things and move before the trap springs — that's fortune. The inferior man waits until retreat is the only option and calls it strategy. You are not the inferior man. You saw this coming. Act like it.

**Line 5** — *Friendly retreat. Perseverance brings good fortune.*

This is the softest of the changing lines and the most precise. Friendly retreat means you don't burn the bridge. You don't need to. The retreat isn't rejection — it's repositioning. You can leave well. You can leave without an enemy. Perseverance here means staying friendly through the leaving, not doubling back to renegotiate.

**Line 6** — *Cheerful retreat. Everything serves to further.*

The last line is the release valve. Cheerful retreat — not grim, not reluctant, not virtuous. Cheerful. Everything serves to further means the retreat itself becomes generative. What you release today comes back as capacity tomorrow. This is not a loss. This is a clearing.

---

**The relating hexagram: Responding (#2)**

When the changing lines complete their movement, Retreat becomes The Receptive — earth over earth, pure yielding, the mare's perseverance. The arc is clear: you retreat from something so you can respond to something else. The primary hexagram asks what you're willing to let go. The relating hexagram shows what opens when you do.

The hexagram underneath all of it is retreat. Not retreat from the work — retreat from the noise around the work. The three of wands wants you scanning the horizon. You can't scan the horizon if you're arguing with someone standing next to you. The retreat clears the sightline.

The seven of pentacles is the assessment phase — you're looking at what's grown and what hasn't, what needs more time and what needs to be pulled. That's the halted retreat of line 3. You want to stay and keep tending. But line 4 says voluntary retreat brings good fortune. You might need to leave some things half-grown. Not everything gets to ripen in your hands.

The knight of pentacles is the friendly retreat of line 5 — slow, steady, no drama. The knight doesn't retreat by running. He retreats by not advancing. He holds his ground but stops pushing forward. That's the posture: still present, still committed, but no longer trying to force the terrain.

Mercury in Cancer in the 11th house says your people already know. You don't need to explain the retreat. Venus in Gemini in the 10th says the public will understand your moves better than you think — your communication has been working even when it felt like it wasn't. The sun in Gemini says don't overcomplicate the message. You're leaving. You're not dying. That's the message.

The relating hexagram, The Receptive, is where this lands. You retreat from the creative push — heaven over mountain — and you land in pure response. Earth over earth. You stop initiating. You start receiving. The mare doesn't lead the herd. She follows the path that's already there.

What this means practically: you have a retreat to make today. It might be from a conversation, a commitment, a role you've been playing. It might be internal — retreat from a thought pattern that keeps you braced for battle. The hexagram says do it before you're forced to. The tarot says you've already done the assessment. The sky says you're clearer than you feel.

Cheerful retreat. Everything serves to further.
