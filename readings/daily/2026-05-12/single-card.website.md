*delays, low energy, false start, hesitation, creative block*

the match is wet. you keep striking it against the box and getting nothing but a faint sulfury smear — the kind of almost-spark that makes you wonder if you even remember how to make a fire. the ace of wands reversed is not a dead idea, it is just a buried one. the potential is still sitting there under the dirt, waiting, but your hands are too tired to dig. this is the false start, the morning you wake up ready to finally begin and then spend three hours staring at the wall. the enthusiasm is there, somewhere, but it is running on fumes. you cannot force a spark in a downpour. you just sit there and let the rain hit the roof until it passes.

## the sky
**Saturn in Aries** (1st house) — saturn is pressing hard on your impulse to just go, demanding a better foundation before you take a single step.
**Mercury in Taurus** (2nd house) — your mind is moving slow, fixed on practicalities, stubbornly refusing to entertain anything that doesn't make immediate, tangible sense.

## what it means together
saturn in aries is the heavy boot on the brake pedal, and mercury in taurus is the voice in the passenger seat asking if you actually checked the map before backing out of the driveway. the sky is not letting you rush, and the card is proving it. that reversed ace is not a personal failure — it is a weather condition. you are trying to light a fire in a damp room with a dull blade and a stern audience. the hesitation you feel is not cowardice. it is self-preservation. saturn demands a real plan, not just a burst of enthusiasm, and taurus wants to know what the return on investment is before it gives you the energy to care. so do not force the start today. let it be a false one. let the idea sit in the dark for another afternoon. the block is the work right now — sitting with the frustration of not beginning, and realizing the delay is the only thing keeping you from building something you will have to tear down next week. rest. plot. wait for dry wood.
