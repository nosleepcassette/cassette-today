*bondage, attachment, materialism, temptation · victory, recognition, confidence, progress · friendship, celebration, community, joy*

The Devil in the past position isn't a ghost story — it's a receipt. Something held you. Maybe it was a job that paid well but cost your sleep. Maybe a relationship built on obligation rather than want. Maybe the belief that you had to earn love through output, or that freedom was something you'd get *after* you finished proving yourself. The Devil doesn't always look like chains. Sometimes it looks like a reasonable compromise you made with yourself, year after year, until you forgot you were the one who agreed to it.

That's where you were. Not bad, not broken — just bound.

And then something shifted. The Six of Wands in present says you're not in that room anymore. You're on the other side of a decision that felt like a risk and turned into a win. People see it. They're acknowledging what you built, what you walked away from, what you held together when it would've been easier to let go. This card doesn't do false modesty. You earned this. The recognition is real. Let yourself have the moment — the Devil taught you how rare genuine progress actually is.

The Three of Cups in future isn't a party invite. It's a reminder that the victory you just secured wasn't meant to be enjoyed alone. This card shows up when the work is done and the people who matter are ready to meet you there. Not for strategy. Not for debriefing. For the kind of joy that doesn't need a reason beyond *we made it*. This is reunion, collaboration, emotional refueling. The kind of gathering where nobody checks their phone because nobody wants to leave.

The morning's King of Swords reversed is worth noting — it's the caution tape. Rigidity, cold judgment, the urge to control outcomes with logic alone. That energy was in the room when you started your day. It wanted you to second-guess the win. It wanted you to keep your distance from the celebration. Don't. The Three of Cups doesn't need a manager.

## the sky

**Venus in Taurus** (11th house) — Venus is at home in Taurus, steady and generous, and she's lighting up your house of friends, networks, and long-term wishes.
**Jupiter in Cancer** (2nd house) — Jupiter expands what it touches, and in your house of value and self-worth, that means abundance isn't just possible — it's the natural outcome of tending what you already have.
**Saturn retrograde in Pisces** (8th house) — Saturn pulls back the veil on shared resources, debts, and the structures you've built around intimacy and trust — time to audit what you're carrying for others.

## the thread

Venus in Taurus in the 11th house is the sky's version of the Three of Cups. It's not coincidental. The universe is stacking the deck for communal pleasure, for friendships that feel like home, for the kind of connection that doesn't drain you because it's built on mutual steadiness rather than performance. You don't have to be interesting to be loved right now. You just have to show up.

Jupiter in Cancer in the 2nd is the Six of Wands' financial echo. Your self-worth is expanding — not because you achieved something, but because you're finally letting yourself *keep* what you've built. The Devil's grip loosens when you stop treating your own value as something that can be taken away. Jupiter in Cancer says: protect your softness. It's your actual currency.

Saturn retrograde in Pisces in the 8th is the asterisk on all of it. The Devil didn't disappear — it transformed. Saturn retrograde asks you to look at the contracts you're still honoring that no longer serve you. Shared accounts. Emotional debts. The things you're holding for other people that they haven't asked you to carry. The King of Swords reversed this morning wanted you to enforce those boundaries with a blade. Saturn retrograde wants you to dissolve them with compassion instead. Different tool, same result: lighter load.

The thread is simple: you broke a pattern, you got the win, and now you get to share the joy. The sky says the people around you are ready for that version of you. The only question is whether you'll let them in before you've finished proving you deserve to be there.
