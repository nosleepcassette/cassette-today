*beginnings, innocence, spontaneity, trust, adventure*

here's the thing about thresholds — you don't get to know what's on the other side before you step through. that's the whole point. the fool shows up when life has stopped asking for your five-year plan and started asking for your willingness. you're standing at a door you didn't build, looking at a path you didn't map, and the only thing you brought is the bag over your shoulder and a vague sense that you're supposed to go.

this isn't naivety. the fool has been around the block — just not this block. the card carries the memory of every fall you've taken and every time you got back up, which is exactly why it's asking you to trust that you can do it again. you don't need to see the whole staircase. you need to see the first step and believe your foot will find the second.

## the sky

no major transits today — the sky is quiet, which is its own kind of message. when the planets aren't screaming, the fool gets room to breathe. the threshold is clear because nothing is clouding it. no retrogrades whispering doubt, no oppositions forcing a choice. just the empty space ahead and the sound of your own feet.

## what it means together

the fool with a quiet sky is a strange gift. it means the leap you're looking at isn't being complicated by cosmic weather — it's just you and the edge. no external pressure to jump, no external reason to hold back. the only variable is your own readiness, and the fool says you've been ready longer than you've admitted.

this is a beginning that doesn't need a fanfare. it might not even feel like a beginning yet. it might feel like a tuesday where you finally say yes to something you've been circling for months. or it might feel like waking up and realizing the door you were waiting for was never locked.

the fool doesn't ask for certainty. it asks for one step. the sky isn't going to make it easier or harder — it's just going to hold space while you figure out whether you trust yourself enough to move.

so. what's the step?
