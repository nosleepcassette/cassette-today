**Four of Cups**

*contemplation, apathy, withdrawal, reevaluation, dissatisfaction*

the four of cups shows up when you've stopped seeing what's right in front of you. not because you're ungrateful — because you're tired. the same well you've been drawing from has gone dry, and someone's holding out a cup you didn't ask for, and you're not sure you have the energy to reach for it.

this card knows the difference between rest and numbness. one is a door. the other is a wall you built because you forgot what you were protecting yourself from. you're not being asked to want the thing you don't want. you're being asked to look up long enough to see whether you're refusing it out of clarity or exhaustion.

## the sky

**moon in scorpio** — the kind of quiet that holds a grudge against itself.

**sun in cancer** — home is where the heart is, but the heart is hiding in the back closet under a pile of old grief.

**saturn in aries** — you've been fighting the same battle for three years and the armor is starting to pinch.

## what it means together

the four of cups meets moon in scorpio like two people in the same room who refuse to speak first. scorpio's moon wants to sit in the dark and turn every feeling over until it's raw. the four of cups doesn't have the energy for that excavation — it just wants the dark to stop moving for five minutes. you're caught between the urge to finally feel something fully and the bone-deep fatigue of having felt too much for too long.

sun in cancer softens the edges but doesn't change the shape. you're allowed to be tender about this. you're allowed to admit that the thing you've been staring at — the hand you keep refusing to shake, the cup you keep pushing away — might actually be something you want, if you could just get past the part where you're mad at yourself for wanting anything at all.

saturn in aries is the kicker. it says you've been grinding on this problem like it's a proof of worth, and the four of cups says maybe that's why you can't see the new thing. you've been so busy being the person who doesn't need anything that you forgot to check whether that person is still you.

the invitation here isn't to force gratitude or manufacture enthusiasm. it's to sit with the dissatisfaction long enough to tell the difference between what's truly not for you and what you've convinced yourself isn't for you because reaching for it would mean admitting you wanted it all along. you don't have to take the cup. but you do have to look at it.
