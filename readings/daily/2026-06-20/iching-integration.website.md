**Union (#8)**

*WATER OVER EARTH · THE ABYSMAL OVER THE RECEPTIVE*

the image is simple: water soaking into earth. not flooding it, not running off — just finding every low place and settling there. that's the instruction. to be the ground that receives, to be the water that finds connection through gravity, not force.

the judgment says *holding together brings good fortune. inquire of the oracle once again whether you possess sublimity, constancy, and perseverance; then there is no blame.*

which is a weird opening move — the oracle asking you to check yourself before you proceed. it's not a blank check for togetherness. it's asking: are *you* ready to hold together? or are you looking for someone else to do the holding while you drift?

union is a water hexagram over an earth hexagram. the abysmal above the receptive. that means the danger is real — water can be treacherous — but earth can absorb it, filter it, turn it into something that sustains. the hexagram names a time when alignment is possible, but only if you're actually present for it. not performing presence. being present.

the image from the ancient kings is instructive: they bestowed fiefs and cultivated friendly relations with feudal lords. that's not sentimental. that's structural. they built a system where loyalty had a container. union without structure is just a crowd. union with structure is a body.

## the changing lines

**Line 2** — *hold to him inwardly. perseverance brings good fortune.*

this is the quiet line. no public declaration, no oath-swearing. just an inner alignment, a decision made in the bones. the line says hold to him inwardly, and the "him" is whoever represents the center of this moment — could be a person, could be your own integrity, could be the work itself. the key is that it's *inward*. you don't need to announce it. you need to mean it.

**Line 5** — *manifestation of holding together. in the hunt the king uses beaters on three sides only and forgoes game that runs off in front. the citizens need no warning. good fortune.*

this is the big one. line five is the ruler's position, and the image is a royal hunt where the king leaves one side open deliberately. he doesn't trap everything. he lets what wants to flee, flee. and the citizens — the people, the relationships, the parts of your life that matter — they don't need to be told to stay. they stay because they want to.

this line is about the difference between forcing cohesion and creating conditions where people choose to cohere. the king doesn't close the net. he trusts that what's meant to be in the circle will stay in the circle. and he's right.

## the relating hexagram

**Multitude (#7)** — *THE ARMY. the army needs perseverance and a strong man. good fortune without blame.*

when the changing lines complete their movement, union becomes multitude. water over earth shifts to earth over water — the receptive over the abysmal. the image flips: instead of water soaking into earth, it's earth containing water. discipline. structure. the army.

this is where the day is going. union, if you do it right — if you hold inwardly and let the net stay open on one side — becomes an organized force. not a mob. not a vague sense of togetherness. a body that can actually move toward something.

the relating hexagram asks: what are you gathering for? union isn't an endpoint. it's the formation stage. multitude is what you do with the people who stayed.

## the sky

**Mercury in Gemini (3rd house)** — your mind is moving faster than the conversation can keep up, and that's fine as long as you're not expecting everyone to catch every leap.

**Saturn retrograde in Aries (10th house)** — the structure you thought was solid is showing its cracks. this is good. you want to know where the foundation is weak before you build on it.

**Venus in Taurus (12th house)** — what you actually long for is quiet, is steady, is something that doesn't require performance. the underground stream. you can feel it even if you can't see it yet.

and then the hexagram says: union.

so here's what's actually happening. the ace of swords wants to cut clean. the three of swords wants to hold the wound. the king of wands reversed wants to put the fire out and walk away. but the hexagram says: don't cut. don't clutch. don't leave. *hold together.*

not the same thing as staying in something that's broken. the hexagram is specific about that — line five says leave the gate open. what wants to go, goes. but what stays? that's your actual body. that's your actual work. that's the people who didn't need a warning to remain.

the mercury in gemini energy wants to talk it all out, explain it, get it right. saturn retrograde says the structure is already shifting under your feet whether you talk about it or not. venus in taurus says what you're really looking for is underneath all of it — a steadiness you don't have to earn.

the relating hexagram is multitude. an army. not a war — an organization. a group that knows what it's for. union is how you find out who's in the group. multitude is what you do once you know.

so the posture for today: hold inwardly. leave the net open on one side. don't chase what runs. and trust that what stays is what was always meant to be in the circle with you.
