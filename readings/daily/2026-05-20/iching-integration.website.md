**Decreasing (#41)**
*mountain over lake, keeping still above the joyous*

the image: at the foot of the mountain, a lake. the water below, the stone above. the lake doesn't overflow its boundary — it decreases, evaporates, gives its moisture upward to the mountain. what is below diminishes so that what is above may be enriched. this is not loss for the sake of loss. this is decrease with a direction.

the judgment says: decrease combined with sincerity brings supreme good fortune. without blame. one may be persevering in this. it furthers one to undertake something. how is this to be carried out? one may use two small bowls for the sacrifice.

two small bowls. not the grand ritual, not the full altar. the hexagram is explicit — you don't need the full apparatus to make a genuine offering. sincerity expressed through simplicity carries more weight than empty ceremony performed with excess. decrease does not mean poverty. it means removing what is unnecessary so that what remains is actually true.

the lake at the foot of the mountain evaporates. the image tells the superior man to control his anger and restrain his instincts. this is not suppression — it is redirection. the energy that would go toward rage or appetite doesn't disappear. it rises. it nourishes the higher ground. anger decreased by keeping still. instincts curbed not by denial but by conscious limitation.

---

the tarot spread reads as a single trajectory: knight of wands in the past, four of wands in the present, temperance in the future. all fire. all upright. no reversals, no hesitation in the cards themselves.

knight of wands as past influence — this is the charge that got you here. the knight rides hard, driven by appetite and conviction, trusting momentum over planning. something began in urgency and passion. maybe it was the right call at the time. maybe the charge was necessary to break through whatever was stuck before. but the knight doesn't pace itself. it burns fuel fast and assumes there will be more.

four of wands in the present — the first moment of solid ground after the charge. this card is homecoming, celebration, stability earned through action. the wands are planted; the canopy is raised. there is something to celebrate. the structure holds. but four of wands is also a threshold — it marks where you've arrived, not where you're going. it's a milestone, not a destination. the party is real but it doesn't last forever.

temperance as the future arc — the alchemist's card. where the knight was pure velocity and the four was pure arrival, temperance says: now you mix. now you find the proportion that sustains rather than combusts. the angel pours between two cups, neither spilling nor hoarding. this is the movement from fire-into-action to fire-into-form. from burning to warming. from charging to composing.

read together, the tarot tells a story of maturation through fire. you started hot. you found ground. now you learn to hold the heat without letting it consume the house you've built.

---

the iching adds depth. hexagram 41, decreasing, arrives at exactly this transition point — the moment where you realize that more effort, more speed, more input is not what's needed. the knight's method won't work from here. what works now is subtraction.

line 6 is changing — the top line of the hexagram, the position of the sage or the one who has already passed through the struggle. it reads: if one is increased without depriving others, there is no blame. perseverance brings good fortune. it furthers one to undertake something. one obtains servants but no longer has a separate home.

this is the paradox of the sixth line in a hexagram about decrease. at the very top — after all the real decrease has happened, after the anger has been restrained and the instincts curbed and the unnecessary stripped away — what remains is someone who can increase without taking. whose enrichment doesn't come at anyone's cost. the two small bowls have become enough to feed everyone.

the relating hexagram is 19, approaching. lake under earth. spring coming. the return of what was given away in the decrease. but it comes back transformed — not as the old abundance that needed to be stripped, but as something lighter, more sincere, more sustainable. approaching means the good is on its way. the hexagram warns only that spring doesn't last forever — which is why temperance in the tarot spread is exactly the right medicine. you don't waste the approach by pretending it will never end.

---

**the synthesis**

may 20 asks you to trust less. not in people — in excess. the knight of wands proved you could move. the four of wands proved you could land. now hexagram 41 asks you to prove you can hold still while something redistributes itself inside you.

this isn't about deprivation. the iching is clear on this: decrease with sincerity is supreme good fortune. decrease without sincerity is just poverty. the difference is whether you're removing what's false or removing what you're afraid to need.

the changing line at position 6 is your exit route — the promise that if you decrease correctly, what you build next doesn't require anyone else's loss. you become someone whose gain is not a zero-sum extraction. that's the real meaning of "two small bowls for the sacrifice." not that you can't afford more. that you don't need more to mean it.

temperance in the future position confirms the direction. the alchemy works. but only if you stop adding ingredients and start finding the right ratio between what's already there.
