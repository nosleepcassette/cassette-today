**Without Falsehood (#25)**
*Innocence, HEAVEN, THUNDER, THE CREATIVE*

Under heaven thunder rolls: all things attain the natural state of innocence — this is the image the hexagram presents. Literally, it describes a scene where the rumble of thunder is heard beneath the vast expanse of heaven, and in this moment, everything reaches a state of pure innocence. This image instructs us to seek a return to innocence, to a state where actions and intentions are pure and unadulterated by falsehood or malice.

The judgment says: *Innocence. Supreme success. Perseverance furthers.* At its core, this judgment is about the power of purity and innocence in achieving success. It's not suggesting naivety or ignorance, but rather a state of being where one's actions are guided by a clear and honest heart. Perseverance furthers because staying true to this path of innocence, despite challenges, is what leads to real success.

The hexagram Without Falsehood names a time of clarity and simplicity, where the posture it demands is one of openness and vulnerability. It sees the situation as one where the removal of deceit and pretenses is necessary for progress. This hexagram is about stripping away the unnecessary and the false to reveal the truth of the matter at hand. It's a call to return to a state of natural innocence, where one can act without the burden of falsehoods and where success is achieved through perseverance in this honest path.

## synthesis
The single card, Three of Cups, set the morning's tone with a sense of community and joy, suggesting that today's successes are rooted in positive relationships and mutual support. The three-card spread, however, showed a more complex psychological arc: from the past's Five of Wands, indicating conflict and tension, to the present's Death (reversed), suggesting a resistance to change or an ending, and finally to the future's The Moon, which brings uncertainty and the need for intuition. The hexagram Without Falsehood, at a structural level, underpins both the tarot readings, emphasizing the importance of innocence and the removal of falsehood in achieving success.

The tarot and the I Ching see the same thing from different angles; both highlight the need for authenticity and clarity. The tarot's focus on relationships and psychological states is complemented by the I Ching's structural and situational analysis. The hexagram complicates the tarot's narrative by adding a layer of moral and philosophical depth, suggesting that the successes hinted at by the Three of Cups can only be truly achieved by embracing innocence and perseverance. The Moon in the future position of the tarot spread adds a layer of complexity, suggesting that while the path forward may be uncertain, it is in embracing this uncertainty with an innocent and open heart that one can find the way.

Practically, this means carrying a posture of openness and honesty into your day. It means recognizing the areas where you may be holding onto falsehoods or pretenses and letting them go. The success and joy hinted at by the Three of Cups are within reach, but they require a commitment to living in a state of innocence and perseverance. This is not about being naive or weak but about being strong enough to be vulnerable and honest. As you move forward, especially into the uncertainty marked by The Moon, remember that your guide is the principle of innocence, and your strength lies in your perseverance.
