*joy, community, friendship, celebration*

you don't have to do this alone — and actually, you shouldn't. the three of cups shows three figures raising their glasses, arms linked, a shared toast to something good. this isn't the card of solitary achievement or quiet victory. it's the one that says the laughter matters more than the outcome, that the point of making something is having someone to show it to.

this card arrives when you've been running on your own fuel for too long. maybe you've been handling things, handling yourself, handling everyone else's expectations — but the three of cups doesn't care about handling. it cares about showing up. it wants you to text the group chat, to say yes to the dinner you were going to skip, to let someone else bring the wine.

there's a particular kind of magic in shared experience that solitary work can't replicate. the three of cups knows this. it's the energy of a good kitchen at midnight, of a project that becomes a party, of healing that happens because someone else is holding the space with you. you don't need to be fixed. you need to be *with*.

## the sky

**no major transits today** — the sky is quiet, which makes the three of cups louder. when nothing is demanding your attention from above, you get to choose where you place your feet.

## what it means together

so here's the thing about a quiet sky and a loud celebration card: you might feel like you should be doing something more serious. like if nothing's happening in the heavens, you should be productive, focused, heads-down. the three of cups disagrees. it says *this* is the work — the gathering, the remembering, the small joy that expands to fill the space you give it.

you've been waiting for permission to connect. here it is.

if there's someone you've been meaning to call, call. if there's a meal you've been meaning to share, share it. if there's a victory — even a small one — that you've been holding quietly, let someone witness it. the three of cups doesn't do grand gestures. it does the real thing: showing up, staying present, letting the good be good without a footnote.

the sky's silence is a gift here. no cosmic drama to distract. just you, and the people who make you feel like yourself, and the simple act of raising a glass to what is, right now, already working.
