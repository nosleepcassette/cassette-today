**Encountering (#44)** 
_trigrams: heaven, wind_ 
_keywords: encountering, heaven, wind, the creative_
the image of encountering describes a situation where wind blows under heaven — a force that stirs, yet is also guided by a higher principle. this image instructs us to approach challenges with a similar balance: to be dynamic and proactive, yet also mindful of the larger context and our place within it. 
"_coming to meet. the maiden is powerful. one should not marry such a maiden_" — this judgment is often misunderstood as a warning against a specific kind of relationship. however, it speaks more broadly to the power dynamics at play in any encounter. the "maiden" represents a force that is both attractive and potentially overwhelming. the advice, then, is not to avoid such forces altogether, but to approach them with caution and a clear sense of one's own boundaries and desires.
encountering names a time of dynamic interaction, where different elements or individuals come together in a way that can be either creative or volatile. it demands a posture of awareness and discernment, recognizing the potential for both cooperation and conflict. in this situation, encountering sees the need for clear communication and mutual respect, lest the encounter devolve into competition or strife.

line 1: "_it must be checked with a brake of bronze. perseverance brings good fortune. if one lets it take its course, one experiences misfortune. even a lean pig has it in him to rage around_" — this line warns against letting situations escalate without intervention. even the most seemingly docile or contained elements can become dangerous if not managed properly. the image of the brake of bronze suggests the need for a strong, yet controlled, response to potential threats.
line 3: "_and walking comes hard_" — this line indicates a point of difficulty or obstruction. progress is not impossible, but it requires more effort than usual. the challenge here is to persevere, even when the path ahead seems uncertain or arduous.
line 4: "_no fish in the tank_" — at first glance, this line might seem enigmatic or unrelated to the situation at hand. however, it suggests a lack or a void that needs to be addressed. whether this refers to a literal absence or a metaphorical one, the message is clear: something essential is missing, and until it is found or created, progress will be hindered.
line 5: "_a melon covered with willow leaves. hidden lines_" — this line is about concealment and the potential for hidden dangers or unseen consequences. the melon, typically a symbol of abundance and fruitfulness, is here covered and obscured, suggesting that not everything is as it seems. caution and vigilance are advised.
line 6: "_he comes to meet with his horns. humiliation. no blame_" — in this final line, the encounter reaches its climax, with one party approaching the other in a forceful or aggressive manner. despite the potential for conflict or humiliation, there is no blame to be assigned, as the situation has unfolded according to its own internal logic.

the relating hexagram is **approaching (#19)**, which asks us to consider how we move towards others, and how others move towards us. it is a hexagram of gradual, respectful approach, emphasizing the importance of patience, persistence, and clarity in all interactions. as the day unfolds, encountering's dynamic, sometimes fraught, interactions give way to a more measured and considerate approach, one that recognizes the value of slow, deliberate movement towards a goal or a connection.
