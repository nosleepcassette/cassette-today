**Approaching (#19)**
*_EARTH, LAKE, THE RECEPTIVE_*

The image of Approaching describes the earth above the lake — a landscape where the solid, dependable earth sits atop the calm, reflective lake. This image instructs us to consider the relationship between stability and depth, where the earth's solidity is not just a foundation but also a boundary that contains and protects the lake's waters. The instruction here is to approach situations with a similar balance in mind, understanding that stability and depth are intertwined.

The judgment of Approaching says: *_APPROACH has supreme success. Perseverance furthers. When the eighth month comes, there will be misfortune._* This message is about the timing and manner of our approach to challenges and opportunities. It suggests that approaching with a mindset of perseverance and a willingness to teach and protect others can lead to great success. However, it also warns of a critical moment in the future (symbolized by the eighth month) when misfortune could arise, indicating that our approach must be consistent and adaptable over time.

Approaching, as a hexagram, names a time of gradual, steady progress towards a goal or a confrontation. It demands a posture of patience, persistence, and an open, receptive attitude. The situation it sees is one where the elements are aligning for a significant encounter or a moment of truth, and it advises us to prepare for this by cultivating our inner strength and our ability to persevere. The emphasis on teaching and tolerance suggests that the approach should be educational, not just for others, but also for oneself, indicating a need for self-reflection and continuous learning.

## the sky
**Mercury in Cancer (12th house)** — Mercury's position in Cancer suggests a strong connection between thoughts, emotions, and the subconscious, indicating that our approach to challenges should be intuitive and considerate of our emotional well-being.
**Venus in Taurus** — Venus in Taurus indicates a focus on stability, reliability, and the appreciation of tangible, sensual pleasures, advising us to ground our approaches in practical, enduring values.
**Mars in Scorpio (2nd house)** — Mars in Scorpio suggests a deep, intense energy driving our actions and desires, pushing us to penetrate to the heart of matters and to transform our relationship with resources and personal values.

## synthesis
The single card, Five of Swords, set the morning's tone with a sense of conflict, possibly internal, and a need to re-evaluate our strategies and defenses. The three-card spread, with The Hermit in the past, Page of Swords in the present, and Death in the future, showed a psychological arc from solitude and introspection, through a period of curious, possibly confrontational, inquiry, towards a profound transformation or ending. The hexagram, Approaching, reveals the structural layer underneath these movements, emphasizing the importance of a gradual, persistent approach, grounded in stability and depth, and warning of the need to persevere and adapt.

The tarot and the I Ching see the same situation from different angles, both pointing towards the necessity of a thoughtful, persistent approach to current challenges. The tarot highlights the psychological and strategic aspects, while the I Ching frames these within a larger, structural context of gradual progress and the importance of timing. They confirm each other in emphasizing perseverance and the need for a deep, reflective approach. The I Ching complicates the tarot's more immediate, psychological insights by introducing the dimension of time and the structural, gradual nature of true progress.

Practically, this means that the person reading this should approach their day with a mindset of steady, persistent effort, balancing between the need for progress and the importance of depth and stability. The action to carry into the day is one of mindful, gradual movement towards goals, with an emphasis on perseverance, self-reflection, and the cultivation of inner strength and tolerance. This posture is not about rapid advancement but about laying the groundwork for supreme success, understanding that true approach is not a momentary act but a process that unfolds over time.
