**Bringing Together (#45)** 
*_lake, earth, the joyous_*

The image of Bringing Together describes a lake situated over the earth — a body of water collected, yet still, on the surface of the land. This isn't just a serene picture; it's an instruction on how to gather your resources, your thoughts, and your people. The lake, as a symbol, represents the accumulation of wealth, ideas, and community, while the earth beneath it signifies stability and practicality. The combination suggests that true strength and success come from organizing and stabilizing your assets, whether they are material, intellectual, or social.

The judgment says: *_Gathering together. Success. The king approaches his temple. It furthers one to see the great man. This brings success. Perseverance furthers. To bring great offerings creates good fortune. It furthers one to undertake something._* This judgment is about the act of coming together, of congregating around a central purpose or figure, and finding success in that unity. It speaks of a king approaching his temple, symbolizing a leader returning to their core values or foundation, and the importance of recognizing and honoring greatness when you see it. The emphasis on perseverance and the act of bringing offerings underscores the idea that success is not a passive state but an active process that requires dedication and the willingness to give and share.

Bringing Together, as a hexagram, names a time of convergence, where different elements of your life are drawn into a singular, coherent whole. It demands a posture of openness, receptivity, and generosity, recognizing that the act of gathering together is not just about accumulating resources but about creating a shared purpose or identity. This hexagram sees the situation as one that requires the integration of disparate parts into a harmonious and functional unit, much like the lake and the earth work together in the image. It's a call to organize, to structure, and to lead, but with the understanding that true leadership is about service and the creation of a common good.

## synthesis
The single card, The Empress, set the morning's tone with themes of nurturing, abundance, and the beauty of nature. The three-card spread, with Strength (reversed) in the past, Two of Swords in the present, and Queen of Wands in the future, showed a psychological arc from a place of inner conflict or blocked strength, to a current state of balance and decision-making, towards a future of inspired action and leadership. The hexagram, Bringing Together, reveals the structural layer underneath these themes, emphasizing the importance of gathering resources, stabilizing foundations, and moving towards a unified purpose.

The tarot and the I Ching see the same moment from different angles, with the tarot focusing on the psychological and emotional journey, and the I Ching illuminating the underlying structural and cosmic patterns. They confirm each other in the emphasis on unity, balance, and the pursuit of a higher purpose. The I Ching complicates the tarot's narrative by adding a layer of collective and societal relevance, suggesting that the individual's journey towards balance and strength is part of a larger movement towards communal harmony and success.

Practically, this means adopting a posture of openness and receptivity, recognizing the value of different perspectives and strengths. It involves organizing your life, not just your schedule or tasks, but your thoughts, emotions, and relationships, to align with a central purpose or vision. The day calls for perseverance, dedication, and the willingness to undertake new endeavors, not as a solitary figure, but as part of a larger whole, moving towards a future where success is not just personal achievement, but a shared prosperity.
