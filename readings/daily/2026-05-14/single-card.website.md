*harmony, family, belonging, peace, fulfillment*

you got the rainbow card. the one with the happy family standing in a green field while ten cups levitate in a perfect arc overhead. it is easy to dismiss this one as a cliché — the tarot equivalent of a stock photo. but that is the cynic's read, and it sells the card short. the ten of cups is not about a fleeting mood or a lucky afternoon. it is about the endurance of belonging. the kind of peace that holds because it is built on something real. chosen kin, the people who see you, the domestic architecture you actually want to come home to. it is the emotional payoff after a long stretch of weather. you stop bracing. you settle.

## the sky
**Venus in Cancer** (4th house) — comfort demands a real foundation, not just a cozy aesthetic

**Jupiter in Gemini** (11th house) — your social circles are expanding, but the real value is in who actually listens

## what it means together
venus moving through cancer is making everything feel a bit more tender, a bit more invested in the sanctuary of home. it wants the soft blanket and the closed door. jupiter in gemini is pulling at the edges of your world at the exact same time, widening the net of who you talk to and how you move through the crowd. the ten of cups sits right at the intersection of these two forces. it asks you to hold both: the deep, private contentment of being truly known by a few, and the sprawling, chaotic warmth of the many. you do not have to choose between the kitchen table and the open road. the harmony you are looking for is not a retreat from the world. it is the feeling of having a place to return to after you have gone out and gotten messy in it. 

the rainbow is not a prize you stumble into. it is the structure you maintain. keep the people who make the quiet feel safe. let the rest of it spin.
